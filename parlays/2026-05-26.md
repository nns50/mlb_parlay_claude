## Full retrospective

**The Strider call was the parlay-saver.** User overrode my "5.5 K at -132 is the
value play" recommendation and bought the deeper 4.5 K alt at ~-625 for safety.
Strider finished with exactly 5 Ks — Over 4.5 cashed, Over 5.5 would have lost.
At +216 the parlay cashes; at +378 (the 5.5-K version) it busts. The user's
variance-respecting choice was correct.

**Where my model was wrong:**
- I had Strider Over 5.5 K at ~72% true. Actual outcome implies the true prob
  was closer to 50-55% — a TJ-return pitcher on a strict pitch-count plan has
  a much harder K ceiling than the 11.0 K/9 raw rate suggests.
- The EV calc said the 4.5 alt at ~-625 was "near fair" (86% market vs ~88% true).
  Today's outcome suggests the 4.5 was probably +EV in reality — book might have
  underpriced the bust scenarios for an elite K arm.
- Net: for high-variance pitcher legs (TJ return, MLB debut, post-IL return,
  pitch-count-managed starts), the deeper alt is the right call even when EV
  math says it's only "fair," because the EV math depends on a true-prob estimate
  that's wider than I model.

**Lesson PROMOTED to CLAUDE.md:** for K-Over legs on pitchers with structural
pitch-count uncertainty (TJ return, debut, IL return, opener-conversion, manager's
documented quick hook), drop one alt deeper than the "value" line even at heavy
juice. The "value" alt assumes a normal start length distribution; these
pitchers' start-length distribution has a left tail the K/9 number can't see.

**What worked elsewhere:**
- Burns Over 6.5 K cashed exactly as the trend suggested (7+ K in 5 of last 6
  starts). The standard-line move (after the 5.5 alt got over-juiced) was correct.
- Dodgers ML cashed despite Lauer's 6.69 ERA being late-discovered — the COL
  weak offense + LAD lineup did the heavy lifting. But the pitcher-swap-ERA
  lesson stands: pre-decision verification of current-season stats is the rule,
  not "the bet won, so it was fine."

**Final score: 1 parlay built, 1 parlay cashed.**
