### Retrospective — calibration lessons

1. **The +200 chase is exactly what lost the ticket.** Every build (A–D) flagged the
   all-ML 2-legger as the *genuine* best chance ("+179 at ~33% is the genuine best chance;
   +289/+278 at ~27% is the best way to actually *clear* +200"). The only reason TB −1.5 RL
   was on the ticket was to manufacture the +200 stamp. That payout knob is precisely what
   busted. This is the **5/30 "don't dress a coin-flip as a best-chance +200" lesson,
   re-validated with money**: when the highest-floor ticket lands just under +200, taking it
   beats forcing a payout knob that drops the floor ~6pp.

2. **A −1.5 run line on a heavy favorite carries FULL ML bust risk PLUS margin risk — it is
   NOT a "safe blowout knob."** The "heavy-mismatch → substitute the −1.5 RL" rule treats the
   run line as a near-lock payout lever (best-AL-record TB vs worst-record DET). But the bad
   team can *win outright*, which loses the RL by the maximum. I even flagged Madden's
   forearm-return variance — but only as a "could keep it low-scoring/close" risk (the wrong
   tail). The realized tail was the opposite and worse: a DET blow-up win. **Refinement: when
   the run line is the only way to reach +200, re-price it as carrying the favorite's full ML
   loss probability (~38-40% here) — not just "fails to cover" risk — and weigh that the
   underdog is a live outright-win team, not dead money.**

3. **Fade-list DET won 10-9 as a DOG — the fade is about fading them AS FAVORITES, and that
   still holds, but laying −1.5 AGAINST a bad team is its own trap.** A 22-38 club is bad
   enough to fade when favored, yet still wins ~35-40% of games outright as a dog — which is
   all it takes to cash +136 and torch a −1.5 laid against them. Don't conflate "fade them as
   a favorite" with "safe to lay runs against them."

4. **I over-shaded MIN down and it cashed clean.** I stacked three cautions on MIN (second
   meeting in 6 days, quietly-hot CWS, 75%-money-on-CWS sharp signal) to rate it ~58-60% and
   prefer SEA as the partner. MIN won 9-6 comfortably. The sharp-money-on-CWS signal did not
   predict the outcome here. Not a rule change (one game), but a calibration note: I may have
   over-penalized a confirmed-ace-at-home (Joe Ryan) on narrative cautions — echoes the 5/28
   "a clear pitching edge beats a recent-form narrative" note. The all-ML SEA+MIN was the
   strongest ticket on the board and I had it identified in every build.
