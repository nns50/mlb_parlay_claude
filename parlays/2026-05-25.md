## Full retrospective
**What worked:**
- All 3 anchor legs landed. Combined true-win-prob estimate of ~33–37%
  was a real edge over the +221 breakeven of 31.2%.
- The alt-K-line rule (promoted this session) played as designed: the
  standard Misi 8.5 +110 was the pure +EV play in isolation (would have
  cashed easily at 12 K) but the 7.5 alt was the right combined anchor
  for parlay safety. Both lines would have cashed today; the alt held
  the combined win probability higher when building the parlay.
- Brewers ML and Misi K were positively correlated (correctly flagged
  pre-game); the correlation paid off — Misi was dominant AND Brewers won.

**What was uncomfortable / worth noting:**
- Dodgers ML at -310 was framed in the original analysis as "the safer
  high-probability choice." User explicitly pushed back: "it wasn't an
  easy leg win as suggested." LAD trailed until the 7th inning before
  winning. The leg cashed but the framing was overconfident — and that
  trust cost is real even when the bet wins.
- Root cause of the framing error: Sheehan's 4.93 ERA was a yellow flag
  at the time of selection. The 74.4% model gap was driven by lineup
  and record, not pitching dominance. -310 implies 75.6% win but says
  nothing about in-game COMFORT, which is a separate dimension users
  care about.
- PROMOTED to CLAUDE.md: never frame a leg as "safe", "easy", or "lock"
  regardless of price. Use the actual win-probability number and
  explicitly flag in-game variance factors (mediocre starter ERA, weak
  bullpen, opponent recent run-scoring form).
